We've identified 64 underdog nations competing in Milan-Cortina 2026 based on several criteria:

  • Limited historical success in Winter Games (few or no medals)
  • Small delegation sizes (often fewer than 5 athletes)
  • Geographic or climatic challenges (nations without natural winter sports infrastructure)
  • First-time or rare Olympic appearances in winter sports

üéØ The Five Underdog Tiers

We've organized nations into five tiers based on the magnitude of their underdog status:

Tier 1: Mild Underdogs
Nations with some Winter Olympic history and occasional medal success, but still competing against more dominant programs.
Tier 2: Moderate Underdogs

Tier 3: Strong Underdogs

Tier 4: Major Underdogs
Countries with extreme obstacles ‚Äì tropical/desert climates, no natural snow, tiny delegations (often 1-2 athletes), and virtually no medal history.
Tier 5: Ultimate Underdogs
The most remarkable stories ‚Äì nations sending athletes despite having no winter sports tradition whatsoever, often competing in their first Winter Games.
